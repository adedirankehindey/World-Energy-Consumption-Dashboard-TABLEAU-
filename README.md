# 🌍 World Energy Consumption Dashboard (2010–2020)-TABLEAU
Interactive analysis of global energy production and consumption trends using Tableau.
Time Period: 2010–2020

📊 Project Overview

This project analyzes global energy production and consumption patterns across countries using a Tableau dashboard built from historical energy datasets.

The analysis focuses on:

• Electricity generation by country
• Oil production trends
• Renewable vs fossil energy consumption
• Global energy distribution patterns

The dashboard provides a high-level analytical view of global energy dynamics between 2010 and 2020.

The project demonstrates how business intelligence tools transform large datasets into actionable visual insights for policy makers, researchers, and energy analysts.

❗ Problem Statement

Energy demand continues to grow globally while governments and organizations attempt to transition toward cleaner and sustainable energy sources.

However, several challenges exist:

• Energy production remains heavily dependent on fossil fuels
• Renewable energy adoption varies significantly by region
• Global energy distribution is uneven across countries
• Decision makers require clear insights into production and consumption patterns

This project aims to answer key analytical questions:

• Which countries dominate global oil production?
• Which regions generate the most electricity?
• How does renewable energy compare with fossil fuels globally?
• What energy sources contribute most to global consumption?

📊 Executive Summary

Key insights from the dashboard include:

Oil Production

Oil production is concentrated among a small group of regions including:

• Middle East
• OPEC countries
• North America

These regions dominate global supply.

Electricity Generation

The highest electricity generation occurs in:

• Asia Pacific
• China
• North America
• Europe

This reflects both population size and industrialization levels.

Energy Mix

Global energy consumption remains heavily dominated by fossil fuels:

Coal, gas, and oil represent the majority of energy usage worldwide.

However, renewable sources such as solar, wind, and hydro are gradually increasing.

Renewable Energy Growth

Renewables contribute significantly through:

• hydroelectric power
• wind energy
• solar energy

But their share remains smaller compared to fossil fuels.
